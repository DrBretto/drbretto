Training Mr. Squiggles to take over the world.

Tech Stack: Placeholder

Here we are training 4 copies of the same AI bot named Mr. Squiggles. Mr. Squiggles doesn't know anything and isn't programmed to do anything. The only difference between each screen is the environment and the goal on which each bot's "fitness" rating is scored:

  1. Top-Left: Distance run to the right (running)
  2. Top-Right: Vertical distance cleared (Jumping)
  3. Bottom-Left: Obstacle avoidance
  4. Bottom-Right: Climbing stairs

In each iteration, 25 Mr. Squiggles bots are generated at the same time (the best one is highlighted). The bots that perform their tasks the best (ie, the "fittest") get to breed. Each new generation, usually, gets a little bit more evolved at solving their task. This is meant to help visualize what's going on with more robust AI neural networks.
